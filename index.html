Visualizing Drug Overdoses in Connecticut

By Madeleine Jin, D'Lani Sweeney, & Olivia Simpson

Connecticut Overdose Heatmap Analysis

Data Interpretation

The interactive heatmap reveals stark geographical disparities in overdose fatalities across Connecticut counties from 2012-2023:

  • New Haven County leads with 2,901 deaths - 24.2% of statewide fatalities
  • Hartford County follows closely with 2,652 deaths (22.1%)
  • Fairfield County accounts for 1,564 deaths (13.1%)
  • The remaining five counties combined represent 40.6% of deaths

Key Findings

The visualization demonstrates three critical patterns:

  1. Urban Concentration: 59.3% of deaths occur in the two most populous counties (New Haven and Hartford), suggesting correlation between population density and overdose incidence
  2. Regional Disparities: The southeast coastal region (New London) shows unexpectedly high rates given its population size
  3. Rural Emergence: While absolute numbers are lower, per capita rates in Windham and Litchfield counties indicate growing rural impacts

Professional Applications

This heatmap serves multiple stakeholders:

Stakeholder Application
Public Health Officials Target harm reduction resources to geographical hotspots
Policy Makers Inform regional funding allocations and prevention strategies
Medical Providers Identify high-risk areas for naloxone distribution and treatment facilities
Researchers Guide further investigation into socioeconomic factors driving spatial patterns

Visualization Takeaway

The heatmap powerfully demonstrates that overdose deaths are not evenly distributed across Connecticut but instead cluster in specific urban centers while showing concerning growth in rural areas. This spatial analysis suggests that while population density is a factor, other elements like economic conditions, access to treatment, and drug distribution networks likely contribute to these geographical patterns. The immediate visual impact of the color gradient makes clear which regions require urgent intervention, while the interactive elements allow professionals to explore specific county data for targeted planning.
